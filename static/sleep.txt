• NREM Stage N1: This is the lightest stage of sleep, which typically lasts for about 5-10 minutes. During this stage, you might experience muscle twitches and your brain begins to produce slower theta waves.

• NREM Stage N2: This stage represents the onset of actual sleep, and it accounts for about 45-55% of total sleep time in adults. Your heart rate and body temperature decrease, and you become less aware of your surroundings.

• NREM Stage N3: Also known as deep NREM sleep or slow-wave sleep, this stage is crucial for feeling rested and restorative. It accounts for about 15-25% of total sleep time in adults. During this stage, your body repairs tissues, builds bone and muscle, and strengthens the immune system.

• REM Sleep: This is the stage where most dreaming occurs. REM sleep typically begins about 90 minutes after falling asleep and recurs every 90-120 minutes throughout the night. Your eyes move rapidly from side to side, heart rate and breathing become irregular, and your brain becomes more active.
